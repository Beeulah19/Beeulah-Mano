ABOUT ME
And This Is Me

I grew up in Tamil Nadu with a dream that had nothing to do with tech — I wanted to be a fashion designer. The way a silhouette moves, how colour creates mood, how composition makes you feel something before you understand why. That eye never left me. It just found a new canvas.

My BTech was in IT. But somewhere between studying systems and watching how people struggled to use them, I found UI/UX design — the place where aesthetic instinct and human empathy meet structure and logic. I didn't go to design school. I learned by doing — designing things, breaking them, connecting with mentors on LinkedIn who became real guides, and iterating until it felt right. Not just looked right.

Values I design by

The way I work is shaped as much by how I think as what I make. These are the principles I come back to when things get complex or ambiguous.

01 Understand before design. I never open Figma before I understand the problem. Who's using this, in what context, under what pressure? The interface is the last thing I think about — not the first.
02 Clarity over cleverness. A beautiful screen that confuses people is a failed screen. I'd rather ship something simple that works than something impressive that doesn't.
03 Own the whole problem. As a solo designer, I learned that design doesn't stop at handoff. I stay involved — in edge cases, in developer questions, in what actually ships. That's where real craft happens.
